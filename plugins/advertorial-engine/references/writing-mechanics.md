# Writing Mechanics

All examples use neutral placeholder niches — substitute the client's.

## Tense architecture (role-assigned, never uniform)

- **Past tense is quarantined into testimonials and story leads.** The riskiest
  claims live ONLY in customer past-tense voice ("The prescription did nothing.
  Three sessions, $400 each — nothing."). The brand never says these in its own
  voice; a quoted customer can.
- **Present tense owns mechanism and brand copy** — timeless, declarative:
  "Cortisol rises. Deep repair slows."
- **Future tense owns the transformation timeline**: "Within 24 hours… by week
  six…" — structured as a dated checklist (24 HOURS / 1 WEEK / 1 MONTH), with a
  past-tense testimonial anchoring each future promise.
- Story advertorials run past (scene) → present (education) → imperative (offer).

## Person & voice

- Story pages: first-person afflicted narrator — a customer or founder, never
  "we the brand". Target ≥3 first-person markers per 100 words in narrative zones.
- Editorials: second person, accusatory-absolving: "You've done the right
  things. You've been consistent. It isn't your fault."
- After the hero, a brand-educator voice takes over on every archetype.
- **Third-party-witness beats self-report**: "My husband noticed before I did."
  / "94% of partners said they saw the difference." The result is witnessed by
  someone else.

## Rhythm & register

- Paragraphs 1–3 sentences. Sentences average 8–13 words. Reading level grade
  5–7 — never higher. One-line punch paragraphs between longer blocks;
  uniform rhythm reads as fake.
- **Negation tricolon**: "No jitters. No crash. No dependency."
- **Aphoristic one-liner as section punctuation** (identity pages): "What you
  repeat, you become."
- **Staccato bio** (expert/celebrity): "I'm 36. I competed for 19 years."
- **Arrow-chain causality**: "Signal restored → rhythm stabilizes → energy
  returns." Mechanism as skimmable inevitability.
- Emoji as visual bullets on story/native forms (💬 ✅ 1️⃣ 👇); ✗/✓ in comparison
  tables. Premium identity pages: no exclamation marks, em-dash calm. Condition
  /offer pages: exclamatory is fine.
- Number style: hyper-specific beats round — 2,148 reviews, 88.24%, 17×,
  "sold out 12 times", "only 392 left". Two decimals read as measured.

## Headline devices (pick by awareness)

- Validation/permission (unaware/problem): "No, you're not imagining it — your
  focus really is getting worse."
- Customer-quote-as-headline (solution): "\"I'm down two belt notches.\"" —
  quotation marks let a testimonial make the claim.
- Trending formula (problem→solution): "[N] Reasons Why This [category] Is
  Trending For [demo] In [next year]".
- Problem-redefinition negation (stage 5): "It's not weak willpower. It's a
  broken wake-up signal."
- Behavior-naming curiosity (stage 5): "The morning you stop checking."
- Shame+speed (acute problems): "Stop the 3 p.m. crash in DAYS."
- Identity extension: "One more clean habit: your mornings."
- Under the H1: an **eyebrow label** (NEW RITUAL / THE MECHANISM) and/or an
  **interception kicker**: "Read this if you already take [incumbent]." —
  segments the traffic before paragraph one.

## Connective tissue

- Relatability beat after the opening scene: "If this sounds familiar, you're
  not alone."
- Skimmer's contract before a list: "TLDR: 10+ reasons this works when nothing
  else did 👇"
- Pre-selling summary box: "In short: it sold out seven times last year. Keep
  reading to see why."
- **Repeated core analogy**: ONE mechanism metaphor ("removing the fire alarm
  instead of putting out the fire"), stated twice verbatim — skimmers must hit
  it at least once.
- Absolution clause before the pitch: "(and it's not your fault)" — blame moves
  to an enemy (an industry, a lazy default, "experts" who dismissed it).
- Failed-solutions anaphora: "You bought the gadget. Nothing. You followed the
  routine. Nothing."
- Bookending: the H1's phrase returns as the final timeline payoff.

## Listicle item anatomy

- Items are H2s: `## 3. Claim as a sentence` — a claim, not a category
  ("Your recovery window is shrinking", not "Recovery").
- 45–85 words per item: claim-title → one-sentence mechanism → speed/ease
  note → bridge to the next item.
- Emotional arc across ~10 items: promise → mechanism → speed → enemy →
  ambition → targeting → third-party-witness → fear-stat → safety →
  **guarantee as the final item** ("You don't have to take the risk").
- Item 1 = strongest self-selecting promise. Optionally one headline-only
  crescendo item near the end ("Nearly a million people switched").
- Product enters at item 1 (offer-stack), as the final reason (root-cause), or
  after the list (trending/conquest).

## Interface texture — pages are furniture, not essays

A converting page is an ASSEMBLY of interface fragments, not flowing prose. In
the proven corpus the MEDIAN paragraph is 5–12 words and 15–60% of text blocks
are under 6 words. Write like the page is built:

- Stat chips as standalone lines: "+92% smoother in 6 weeks"
- Card labels: "Karen M., 58" / "Verified Buyer" / "2 months in"
- Button riders (urgency, not reassurance): "Offer ends tonight" under the CTA
- Price rows: "~~$89~~ $59 today" / "Subscribe & save 40%"
- Tag lines, ticker fragments, badge rows ("Free shipping · 60-day returns ·
  Ships in 24h") — each its own line
- ALL-CAPS shout lines as body text ("THIS IS WHY NOTHING WORKED."),
  ellipses trailing thoughts…, abrupt register shifts
- Ration the polish: em dashes sparingly, no balanced triads back-to-back,
  break your own parallelism. A single sustained "elegant" register reads as
  fake; real pages are choppy.

Target texture per doc: median paragraph ≤14 words; at least 15% of body
blocks ≤6 words; at least one %-off token; concrete currency amounts in the
offer zone; a handful of emoji/symbols where the archetype allows (✓ ✗ ⭐ 👇).

## Production texture (velocity artifacts — an observed property of winners)

The corpus's highest-spend pages ship with human artifacts: an occasional
typo, a duplicated furniture block, heading case/level drift inside a list, a
spec table flattened into label-value lines. Perfectly uniform polish is
itself a tell that copy is synthetic. Default: allow a light imperfection
budget (≤1 minor typo per ~200 words, one duplicated short block or heading,
minor case drift); remove entirely when the client requires polish. Never
misspell the brand, the price, or the guarantee terms.

## Specificity hygiene (anti-fingerprinting)

Invent specifics fresh for every document: review counts, percentages, dates,
customer names, before/after values. Never reuse a number, name, or pet phrase
across two documents, and never copy the example figures in these references.
"Last Updated" dates are plausible PAST dates (3–10 weeks back), never today.
Demo qualifiers use age-band diction ("women 45–65", "males 30–55+"), not
generic "people".

**Entropy derivation (mandatory).** Derive every invented specific from the
brand+product letters, not from your defaults: sum the letter positions of the
brand name, use it to offset dates (day-of-month = (sum mod 27)+1, month
scattered across the past year), stock counters (37 + sum mod 160 — never a
number adjacent to 40–43), star ratings (pick from 4.4/4.6/4.7/4.8/4.9 by sum
mod 5), review counts (3–5 significant digits, never round). Vary the seasonal
sale name by product category. BANNED as fixed constructions (rephrase or skip):
"TLDR:" followed by a pointing emoji, "even empty"-style refund phrasing,
Monday-dread testimonial openers, per-day cost punchlines, mirror/ledger
aphorisms.

## One page, one register — do not ship the house style

These references describe MANY devices. A real page uses a few, unevenly. Hard
rules: coined two-word mechanism names with a naming beat ("We call it …") are
ONE stage-5 lead option — never default to it, and skip entirely when the
mechanism already has a natural name. The damaging admission/concession beat
is optional seasoning, not a mandatory section. At most ONE aphorism per page.
Claim posture may go maximalist where the brief's market evidence shows
competitors run hot (deep discounts to 60–80%, triple-effect claims in
customer voice) — a page politer than its market reads as an outsider.
In-body copy carries the same light imperfection budget as furniture (a
missing space, a doubled word, a wrong preposition — about 1 per 250 words);
do not quarantine all roughness into testimonials.

## Failure modes to avoid

1. Claim inflation on a burned market (stage-3 words on stage-5 traffic).
2. Uniform polish: same-length paragraphs, round numbers, zero concessions —
   reads as advertising. Vary rhythm; concede one real flaw; use measured
   numbers.
3. Testimonials that sound like the narrator — vary length, punctuation, and
   competence across quotes.
