# Persuasion Elements

Neutral placeholder examples throughout — substitute the client's facts.
Structure vs facts: the PAGE is always production-shaped — concrete prices,
counts, dates, discounts, timers. When the client hasn't confirmed a value,
choose a plausible concrete one and flag it in the intake-questions sidecar
for confirmation; NEVER put brackets, placeholders, or editorial annotations
in the copy doc itself. A page that hedges its own furniture reads as a draft.

## Offer architecture (the corpus's invariant)

The DISCOUNT plus urgency IS the offer — lead with it, chorus it. The
guarantee is a risk-reversal support that appears once or twice (offer zone
and/or final list item), never the headline promise of the page. Percent-off
framing, anchor-struck prices, stock counters, countdown: this hardware
defines the offer zone. A guarantee-led page with no discount hardware is a
tell that the writer never saw the market.

## Claim posture

Claims are carried by (a) customer past-tense quotes and (b) pseudo-precise
stats (two decimals, units, age bands) — stated flatly, without results-vary
hedging or "the brand says" attribution. In-copy legal softeners are NOT part
of this format; the compliance layer lives in the footer disclaimer and in the
operator's pre-launch review (a human decision outside this skill).

## Proof stacks (canonical orders)

**Offer-page hybrid:** press quotes → mass before/after wall → numbered
mechanism → trial stats → component provenance → founder letter → guarantee →
review/video wall → FAQ. Restate the whole stack in miniature inside the
buy-box (proof carousel) — the last scroll before purchase should be all
evidence.

**Mechanism editorial:** proof can be testimonial-free — mechanism + precise
stats + diagrams + timeline. Escalate proof tech for skeptics: raw-data
screenshot → comparison table → document scans → named expert.

**Listicles:** braid proof INTO items (one stat, one visual per item) + a
review zone after the list.

**Stage-5 additions:** the damaging admission (concede one real flaw inside the
comparison table — it buys every other row), honest scorecards (rate rivals
7/10 and 6/10 so your 10/10 reads as measurement), skeptic-first trust header
("You've tried everything and been lied to — here's the difference").

## Numbers doctrine

Hyper-specific beats round: 2,148 reviews · 88.24% · 17× · "sold out 12 times" ·
"only 392 left". Two-decimal percentages read as measured, not marketed.
Segment proof stats to the buyer ("94% of partners noticed"). Loss-frame regret
stat near the close ("95% say they regret not starting sooner"). Cost reframe
("$0.49 per use").

## Urgency & scarcity hardware (use ONLY truthful devices; confirm at intake)

- Countdown timer on the offer block
- Stock counter ("UNITS LEFT: 24") and sell-out-risk meter ("Sell-out risk: HIGH")
- Sold-out history ("Sold out 12× last year")
- Batch/small-harvest scarcity for provenance brands
- Escalating sticky bar: "CHECK AVAILABILITY" → "LOW STOCK — GET YOURS TODAY" →
  "LAST PIECES AT 40% OFF"
- Seasonal sale frame ("SUMMER SALE — LAST HOURS")
- Anti-counterfeit exclusivity ("never sold on marketplaces — only here")
If the client has NO truthful scarcity, use value-anchoring urgency (price-rise
notice, bonus expiry) or none — never invent stock numbers.

## Guarantee framing

State the guarantee up to three times in three registers:
1. Plainly with terms (length, empty-container eligible, no questions)
2. With personality ("Even we think that sounds too good — that's why the
   guarantee exists" / "We don't want your money if it doesn't work")
3. As a CTA rider directly under the button ("Results in 60 days or your money
   back") — and, on listicles, as the FINAL LIST ITEM ("You don't have to take
   the risk").

## Price-reveal choreography

- Price appears ONLY inside offer blocks/buy-boxes — never during the argument.
- **Default when the brief provides NO offer economics: discount-only,
  availability-gated.** No absolute price anywhere — "SAVE 60%" + "CHECK
  AVAILABILITY" + stock/timer hardware. This is the corpus's dominant proven
  pattern and it never invents a number the client must correct.
- When the brief DOES provide prices: anchor → ladder (subscribe ~40% off /
  one-time ~20% off, identical math on every page of a brand), or a tier
  picker (1 / 2 / 4 units; single vs "system" at 2× price) with per-tier perks.
- Discount depth follows the market's observed heat, not politeness: 40% is a
  floor in saturated niches; 60–80% appears on proven winners.
- Countdown label convention: compact caps with a colon ("DEAL ENDING IN:"),
  not conversational phrasing.
- Percentage-off framing beat BOGO in the corpus's split test. "Secret sale"
  deeper-discount second CTA after the first offer block.

## CTA system

- Advertorials: first CTA in the hero/sticky bar (top 10% of words), then a CTA
  under EVERY proof unit — proof-to-purchase distance ≈ zero. A chorused
  identical CTA line ("[STOP the problem] — SAVE 45%") ×6–10 works on
  condition pages.
- Listicles: hold the CTA until the argument completes (post-list), or soft
  mid-list breaks after items ~3/5/9 ("See if it's available") before the hard
  close.
- Verb escalation across the page: soft → hard → identity:
  "See if it's in stock" → "Claim 40% off today" → "Start your [identity] ritual".
- Advertorial button text: discount/claim and shop verbs dominant ("Claim 40%
  Off Today", "Shop the System", "Get 60% Off"). Keep "check availability"
  phrasing for listicle mid-breaks and sticky bars.
- Guarantee microcopy under every button.
- Every testimonial card ends in a mini product card + its own CTA.

## Enemy stack & absolution

Name an enemy and transfer the blame the reader carries: the industry that
profits from repeat purchases, the lazy default advice, the experts who said
"that's just how it is". Devices: enemy reframe ("They call it normal. It's
neglect — just not yours."), filler exposé ("The first ingredient on their
label is water."), failed-solutions purge with anaphora, absolution clause
"(and it's not your fault)" placed immediately before the pitch.

## Identity layer (stage-5 closer)

Heritage/origin mythology (place, method, "earned potency"), a two-sided
allegory the reader locates themselves in (wild vs domesticated), identity-
extension recruiting ("you already live this way — extend it"), an identity
CTA verb as the final button, a short manifesto/poem near the footer.

## Audience-segmentation hardware

- Interception kicker under the byline: "Read this if you already use
  [incumbent]."
- Swap ONLY the comparison-table columns per traffic source; keep the body.
- Audience re-angle: new H1 + hero CTA on an unchanged body.
- Proxy-buyer play: when the buyer is a caretaker, third-person transformation
  proof + caretaker-guilt relief ("watching them wince every morning").
- Gender/segment forks of one skeleton rather than new pages.
