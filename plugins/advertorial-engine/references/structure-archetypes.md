# Structure Archetypes — flow maps with word budgets

Derived from a 73-page corpus of proven direct-response landing pages,
ads-weighted (heavier-spend pages counted as stronger evidence). All examples
below use neutral placeholder niches; substitute the client's.

## The master principle: pages are assembled, not composed

Winning pages are built from a reusable block library: press-quote wall,
before/after testimonial card wall, numbered mechanism block, comparison table,
expectation timeline, buy-box with proof carousel, countdown + stock counter,
founder letter, guarantee block, video/review wall, FAQ. A "new page" is usually
the same skeleton with 3–5 swapped slots (hero angle, byline persona,
interception quote, comparison-table columns, offer depth). Build the skeleton
once; angle it many times.

## ADVERTORIAL ARCHETYPES

### ADV-1 · Offer-page hybrid — the highest-spend money page
Advertorial argument fused onto the product offer. For product/most-aware traffic.
Budget: 1,500–2,600 body words · 25–45 image slots · 8–14 CTAs · first CTA in hero.

1. Hero: promise headline + social count ("Join 88,000+…") + primary CTA (60–120w)
2. Press-quote wall: 3–6 one-line endorsements (30–60w)
3. Mass proof wall: 4–8 before/after testimonial cards, each = photo pair +
   name/age + two goal tags + short quote + product card + CTA (150–400w)
4. "Backed by science" numbered mechanism: 3–5 numbered blocks (150–300w)
5. Trial/stat block with decimal-precision numbers (80–200w)
6. Scarcity headline ("Sold out 12× last year") (20–40w)
7. Ingredient/component proof cards with origin + stat chip each (150–250w)
8. BUY BOX: anchor price → subscribe ~40% off / one-time ~20% off + low-stock
   warning; a proof carousel restates the whole argument here (100–200w)
9. Founder/origin letter (100–250w)
10. Guarantee, personality-framed (60–120w)
11. Review/video wall header + FAQ

Persona: brand voice. NO byline, NO date.

### ADV-2 · Personal-story advertorial
Afflicted-narrator "I" article. For unaware/problem-aware colder traffic.
Budget: 1,800–2,800 words · 25–45 images · 8–14 CTAs · first CTA ≤5% via
sticky/hero, first INLINE CTA after the relatability beat.

1. Symptom scene, past tense, concrete and somatic (90–150w)
2. Relatability beat: "If this sounds familiar… you're not alone." (30–60w)
3. Expert-consult turn (a professional names the real cause) (60–120w)
4. The reveal/diagnosis (80–150w)
5. Mechanism education, numbered, present tense (180–300w)
6. Failed-solutions purge — everything the reader already tried, and why each
   fails mechanically (80–150w)
7. Product entry as a SYSTEM (not a thing) (120–200w)
8. How-to + benefit stack, ending in the guarantee (150–250w)
9. Review wall

First-person density ≥3 per 100 words in the narrative zones. Byline optional —
the corpus's proven forms mostly skip it; add only if the client's placement
expects a named author.

### ADV-3 · Mechanism editorial (problem-redefinition)
For solution-aware or burned stage-4/5 traffic. Budget: 1,400–2,200 words ·
20–40 images · 5–10 CTAs · first CTA ≤10%.

1. Eyebrow label + negation/curiosity headline ("It's not X. It's Y.") (20–60w)
2. Problem redefinition or named-pattern coinage — invent a nameable diagnosis
   and teach its loop (150–300w)
3. Evidence block: cited or precise stats (150–250w)
4. The reframe sentence (a one-liner that relocates the problem) (60–150w)
5. Numbered mechanism with ONE core analogy, stated twice verbatim (200–350w)
6. Transformation timeline (24 hours / 1 week / 1 month), a testimonial
   anchoring each phase (150–300w)
7. Failed-alternatives lecture ("why 99% of X fail") (150–250w)
8. Product-as-answer + flash-sale close: countdown, stock counter, guarantee
   (150–300w)

Variant ADV-3c · celebrity/expert-ritual: open with credentials kicker
(name • title • record) → staccato first-person bio → the figure personally
teaches the mechanism ("A note from…") → habit framing ("non-negotiable part
of my daily routine… 30 seconds a day") → continue the master body. Real
photos only.

### ADV-4 · Identity/values editorial
For values-driven solution-aware audiences at stage 5. Budget: 900–1,600 words,
quiet register, low urgency.

1. Identity affirmation ("You've built your habits carefully.")
2. Values pivot with a quiet fear appeal (the overlooked gap)
3. ✗/✓ comparison table (them vs us)
4. Provenance-labeled component cards
5. Single strong stat + CTA
6. Ritual grid (morning / evening / weekly)
7. Buy-box, one urgency block max

Aphoristic one-liners punctuate sections. No exclamation marks.

### ADV-5 · Visual-panel advertorial
Copy at caption scale; panels argue. Budget: 700–1,000 words · image every
~20–30 words · 8–14 short sections · 4–12 CTAs. Same section ORDER as ADV-1;
every section = 1–3 short lines + a panel (comparison strip, mechanism card,
proof collage). Use when the audience skims (mobile-first cold traffic).

## LISTICLE ARCHETYPES

### LIS-1 · Trending/N-reasons listicle — the listicle money page
For problem→solution-aware traffic. Budget: 1,000–1,700 words · N = 10–12
items, 45–85 words each · images ≈1/item · CTA post-list OR soft mid-list
breaks after items ~3/5/9.

1. Sale banner + countdown (evergreen)
2. H1: "[N] Reasons Why This [category epithet] Is Trending For [demo] In [year]"
3. Byline: named peer or credentialed persona + "Last Updated" date
4. Interception quote targeting the segment: "Read this BEFORE your next …"
5. 3-column alternatives-defeat table (product vs the two options THIS audience
   is weighing; swap columns per traffic source, keep the body)
6. TLDR skimmer's contract ("…10+ life-changing benefits 👇")
7. Items 1–N, fixed emotional arc: promise → mechanism → speed → enemy →
   ambition → targeting → third-party-witness → fear-stat → safety →
   **guarantee as the final item**
8. Hard offer close: % off + timer + stock counter + sell-out risk + guarantee

Persona: byline + fresh date, ~100% in the corpus.

### LIS-2 · Conquest/comparison listicle
For product-aware users of a NAMED incumbent. Budget: 1,100–1,700 words ·
10–11 items · one end offer.

1. Allegory or concept-flip essay open (two-animal allegory; "treating vs
   masking") (150–250w)
2. Story testimonial with skepticism inoculation ("I didn't believe it either")
3. Comparison table WITH a damaging admission (concede one real flaw)
4. Multiplier table ("4× the [nutrient] of [incumbent] — 220 mg vs 60")
5. Items sell the CATEGORY switch: mechanism early, emotion mid, speed late
6. Headline-only crescendo item ("Almost a million switched")
7. Transformation-checklist close → single end offer block

Do NOT trim the identification ramp — the long frame outspends the short one.

### LIS-3 · Hero-offer selling-point stack
For product/most-aware retargeting. E-com hero with dual CTA BEFORE item 1 →
5 objection-collapse items (mass-proof → mechanism → targeting → simplicity →
guarantee) → the SAME offer block repeated after items 1/3/5 → review wall.
600–1,100 words.

Variant LIS-3c · ranked confession: first-person "Top 5 things that actually
worked for me"; item 1 = the product with a scorecard (10/10, A+); items 2–4 =
honestly-scored weaker alternatives (7/10, 6/10, 5/10); item 5 = unscored
emotional plea. The honest low scores make the 10/10 believable.

### LIS-4 · Root-cause argument listicle
For skeptical problem-aware traffic at stage 5. Invent a diagnosis ("We call it
[coined term]"), then N reasons structured as kill-shots on the familiar
solutions, product enters as the final reason. CTA interrupts at items ~4/8.

## PAGE FURNITURE (mandatory on every page — a page without its furniture is a draft)

Real deployed pages carry commerce furniture end to end. Include ALL of it:

1. **Sale banner near the top**: seasonal frame + percent-off ("SUMMER SALE —
   UP TO 40% OFF") and a countdown token line ("Ends in 04 : 23 : 11" — timers
   render in mixed states; a zeroed "00 : 00 : 00" is normal).
2. **Sticky-bar CTA lines** (as standalone short lines near the top and again
   mid-page): the SAME short all-caps CTA string.
3. **Buy-box block** (offer zone): when the brief provides offer economics —
   anchor price struck to offer price in the client's currency,
   subscribe-vs-one-time rows, per-tier perk bullets. When it does NOT —
   availability-gated: discount percent + "CHECK AVAILABILITY"-class CTA, no
   absolute prices. Either way: low-stock warning ("UNITS LEFT: 118"),
   sell-out-risk line, guarantee rider, countdown labeled compact-caps
   ("DEAL ENDING IN:").
4. **Review-wall zone**: platform-chrome review dump — star row, count line
   ("4.7 ★ · 2,318 reviews"), 2–4 review cards as short-line clusters (name,
   age · date · platform tag · benefit tag · rambling quote — length-varied,
   imperfect — · "Shop now" line). On listicles keep at most 2 as blockquotes;
   the rest are line clusters, not quotes.
5. **FAQ stub** (3–5 Q lines with 1–2-line answers) on offer hybrids.
6. **Footer shell** (every page, last lines): links line
   ("Terms of Sale · Privacy Policy · Shipping · Contact"), copyright line
   ("© [year] [Brand]. All rights reserved."), and the category's standard
   regulator disclaimer paragraph (e.g., for supplements the FDA-evaluation
   statement; for other categories the equivalent results-may-vary/consult
   line). The disclaimer is footer furniture — it does NOT belong in body copy.

**Verbatim block repetition is a page FEATURE, not an error.** Real pages are
assembled from modules, and modules repeat literally: the ENTIRE offer block
(banner line + discount + timer + stock + CTA + rider) appears verbatim 2–3
times (after items ~1/3/5 on listicle stacks; mid-page and close on
advertorials); the sticky-bar CTA line recurs at intervals as a standalone
line; the review-wall header (star row + count) recurs where proof reappears;
a key mechanism sentence may be restated word-for-word. Copy the repeated
blocks LITERALLY — do not paraphrase a module on its second appearance.

CTA economy: ONE short all-caps CTA string reused verbatim throughout
("CHECK AVAILABILITY", "GET 60% OFF") — 4–10 occurrences; button texts never
contain em dashes and never exceed ~5 words; microcopy UNDER buttons is
urgency/stock, with the guarantee rider appearing there only in the offer zone.

## STRUCTURAL GUARDRAILS (format-level, hard checks before delivery)

| rail | advertorial | listicle |
|---|---|---|
| body words | 1,200–2,800 (never >4,500) | 900–1,700 (NEVER >1,800) |
| H2 sections | 8–24 | items 8–12 (H2 `## N. Title`) |
| image slots | ~1 per 40–70 words (min 12) | ~1 per item + 2–4 in offer zone |
| CTA blocks | 6–14; FIRST within top 10% of words | 2–8; post-list or mid-list breaks |
| CTA phrasing | discount/claim + shop verbs dominant | any mix; soft "check availability" allowed mid-list |
| testimonial blockquotes | 5–11 (with attribution dashes) | 0–2 |
| stat mentions | 8–30 | 4–14 |
| byline / date lines | NO / NO | YES / YES ("Last Updated …") |
| DISCLOSURE line | omit unless client requires | omit unless client requires |
| image captions | `caption: none` (adjacent one-line paragraphs do caption work) | same |
| questions | sparing (≤2 per 100 sentences) | sparing (≤5 per 100) |
| reading level | grade 5–7, sentences avg 8–13 words | same |
| paragraph texture | median ≤14 words; ≥15% of blocks ≤6 words | same |
| offer texture | ≥1 %-off token; currency amounts ONLY if brief gives offer economics (else availability-gated, no prices) | same |
| body words incl. furniture | keep total ≤2,800 | keep total ≤1,700 |
| assumptions | NEVER bracketed in the copy doc (sidecar only) | same |
