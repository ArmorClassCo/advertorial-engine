# Context Checklist — what this skill needs before writing

The quality ceiling of an advertorial or listicle is set by the context you feed it,
not by the writing. Collect every item below before drafting. Items marked ★ are
blocking: without them the page will be generic and the strategy declaration will be
a guess.

## 1. Product & mechanism ★
- What the product is (format, ingredients/components, usage, dosage/frequency)
- The mechanism story: WHY it works, and — more powerful at high sophistication —
  why the things the prospect already tried FAIL (mechanism-of-failure)
- Time-to-results the client considers defensible
- SKU/bundle structure available to sell

## 2. Audience & awareness ★
- Demographic (age band, gender skew, life stage) and the emotional trigger moments
  (the concrete scenes where the problem bites)
- What they have already tried and how they explain those failures to themselves
- Prospect awareness level of the TRAFFIC this page will receive
  (unaware / problem-aware / solution-aware / product-aware / most-aware)
- Vocabulary they use for the problem (their words, not clinical words)

## 3. Market sophistication evidence ★
- How long this market has been sold this product category; which claims are
  burned out; which mechanisms competitors already lead with
- 3–5 competitor angles currently running (what the prospect has already seen)
- Stage estimate 1–5 with the evidence (see references/schwartz-mapping.md)

## 4. Offer & price architecture ★
- Unit price, bundle tiers, subscription option, discount depth
- WHICH offer this page should push (the page choreographs one primary offer)
- AOV target / bundle economics if bundles are pushed

## 5. Guarantee & risk reversal ★
- Guarantee length and exact terms (empty-container eligible? conditions?)
- Any other risk reversals (free shipping, free returns, try-before-pay)

## 6. Funnel & traffic context ★
- Traffic source and placement (native, Meta, TikTok, YouTube, email) — this sets
  hook style, disclosure norms, and how "editorial" the disguise may be
- CTA destination (product page / cart / quiz) and whether the page hosts the offer
- Device skew (mobile-first changes image cadence and paragraph length)

## 7. Proof inventory ★
- Verbatim customer quotes WITH permission status (names/photos usable?)
- Review counts and average rating that may be cited
- Studies, surveys, or pilot data with the exact numbers usable in copy
- Expert/authority figures attached to the brand (title, how they may be
  named/pictured)
- Before/after or UGC visual assets that exist

## 8. Substantiation boundaries & compliance
- Claim rules for this category (e.g., supplement structure/function limits,
  no disease-treatment claims), required disclaimers
- Whether editorial-disguise styling (byline, publication framing, dateline,
  "ADVERTORIAL" label) is acceptable to the client — this is an operator/compliance
  decision made by a human, not by this skill
- Words or claims the client's legal/compliance has banned

## 9. Founder / origin story
- Who created the product and why; the personal stakes; anything that supports a
  story-lead or a trust section

## 10. Brand voice constraints
- Style guide, banned words, tone boundaries, how aggressive competitor-negging may be

## 11. Visual asset inventory
- Product shots, lifestyle photos, UGC, diagrams that already exist vs. what must
  be specified as new-image briefs (this skill outputs full image specs either way)

## 12. Truthful urgency levers
- Real stock limits, batch constraints, seasonal windows, planned price changes —
  only honest scarcity may be used; if none exists, the page uses value-anchoring
  urgency instead

## 13. Objections & FAQ data
- Top pre-purchase questions from support/chat
- Top refund reasons (each is an objection the page must pre-empt)

## 14. Usage specifics
- Dosing/usage by segment if relevant; safety or interaction disclaimers

---

## Degraded-context protocol

- **Full context** (all ★ items): proceed to strategy declaration and generation.
- **Partial context** (some ★ missing): output the numbered list of missing items as
  questions to the client, phrased concretely. If instructed to proceed anyway,
  generate with clearly marked placeholder assumptions (e.g., `[ASSUMED: 60-day
  guarantee — confirm]`) and list every assumption at intake time.
- **Minimal context** (product name and little else): do not guess a strategy, do
  not write copy. Emit TWO deliverables that run in parallel, then stop:
  1. The deep-research prompt (intake/deep-research-template.md, filled with what
     is known) — for everything the open web can answer (market sophistication,
     competitor angles, audience language, mechanism science, category price and
     compliance norms).
  2. The client question list — the missing ★ items ONLY THE CLIENT can answer,
     phrased concretely: their actual price/offer economics, guarantee terms,
     funnel destination and traffic source, permission-cleared testimonials and
     visual assets, truthful urgency levers, founder story, brand voice rules.
     Mark each with why research cannot substitute for it.
  Research and client answers arrive independently; generation begins when the ★
  categories are covered by either source.
