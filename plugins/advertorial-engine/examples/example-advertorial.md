## What the wake-up loop actually is

Around 3 a.m., your deepest sleep is already finished. Sleep runs lighter from there.

Light sleep is easy to break.

In midlife, cortisol reactivity climbs. In perimenopause it can spike hard enough to soak a nightgown.

Then the loop closes:

Cortisol rises → you surface → the mind switches on → being awake stresses you → more cortisol.

If night sweats visit you too, they and the loop feed each other. A hot flush is a cortisol event, and cortisol is a wake event.

One spike. Two hours awake.
